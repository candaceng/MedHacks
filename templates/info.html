Learn more about the different types of skin lesions!

Actinic Keratoses

A very early form of skin cancer that's easily treatable. The main sign is a red, scaly patch on the skin.

Learn More
Basal Cell Carcinoma

These cancers usually start in the basal cell layer and develop on sun-exposed areas like the face and neck.

Learn More
Benign Keratosis

A common noncancerous skin growth. They look waxy, scaly and slightly raised. They appear on the head, neck, chest or back.

Learn More
Dermatofibroma

Harmless growths within the skin with a small diameter. Feels like small stones underneath or raised above the skin.

Learn More
Melanoma

The most serious type of skin cancer. A change in the colour, size or shape of a mole is usually the first sign of melanoma.

Learn More
Melanocytic Nevi (Moles)

Benign neoplasms or hamartomas composed of melanocytes, the pigment-producing cells in the epidermis.

Learn More
Angiomas

Benign tumors derived from cells of the vascular/lymphatic vessel walls. Might indicate systemic problems like liver disease.

Learn More
Pyogenic Granuloma

Skin growths that are small, round, and usually bloody red in colour. They tend to bleed due to a large number of blood vessels.
